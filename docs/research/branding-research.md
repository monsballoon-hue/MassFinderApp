# MassFinder Branding Research — App Name & Identity

**Date:** 2026-03-15
**Researcher:** Claude Opus (product research)
**Trigger:** Developer questioning whether "MassFinder" still fits the app's expanded scope

---

## Problem Statement

MassFinder launched as a Mass-times finder for Western New England. It now includes:

- **Service discovery:** Mass, Confession, Adoration, events across 93 parishes
- **Prayer tools:** Guided Rosary, Examination of Conscience, Stations of the Cross, Novena Tracker, First Friday/Saturday tracker, Divine Mercy Chaplet
- **Deep formation:** Full Catechism (2,865 §§), DRB + CPDV Bible, Baltimore Catechism, Lectionary Index, Faith Guides
- **Liturgical living:** Seasonal content (Holy Week guides, O Antiphons, monthly devotions, liturgical color awareness, Easter/Advent cards), daily readings, saint of the day
- **Parish curation:** Saved parishes dashboard, today timeline, community life events

"MassFinder" describes roughly 15% of what this app does. The name has also collided with existing products:

- **massfinder.app** — already registered and active (a competing US Mass directory)
- **massfinder.org** — another Mass-finding service (zip code based)
- **Catholic Mass Times** — the 800-pound gorilla (130,000+ churches worldwide, 20K+ App Store reviews, featured by EWTN/Vatican News)
- **MassTimes.org** — 501(c)(3) backed by Diocese of Lansing, 25 years running

Competing directly in the "find Mass" namespace against these established players is unwinnable and undersells what MassFinder actually is.

---

## Competitive Landscape — Naming Patterns

| Category | Examples | Pattern |
|----------|----------|---------|
| Generic descriptive | Catholic Mass Times, Catholic Missal 2026, Daily Catholic Readings | Function + year. Forgettable. Ad-infested clones. |
| Single evocative word | **Hallow**, **Laudate**, **Amen**, **Verbum**, **Magnificat** | One resonant word. Brandable. The winning pattern. |
| Function-specific | iBreviary, Click To Pray, Pray As You Go | Verb-driven. Works if scope is narrow. |
| Parish-centric | myParish App | Clear but limits to parish context. |

**The winners all use Option B:** Hallow (from "hallowed be thy name"), Laudate (Latin: "praise"), Amen, Verbum (Latin: "the Word"). These names are memorable, emotionally resonant, don't constrain scope, and signal Catholic identity without being clinical.

## Three Options

### Option A: Keep "MassFinder"

**Pros:**
- Existing users know it
- Clear about the core utility

**Cons:**
- Collides with massfinder.app (active competitor), massfinder.org, and the massive Catholic Mass Times ecosystem
- Describes ≤15% of current functionality
- Signals "utility" not "companion" — doesn't invite lingering, prayer, formation
- Locks the brand to "finding" when the app is increasingly about "dwelling"
- Cannot acquire massfinder.app or massfinder.com — both active
- Confusing SEO/word-of-mouth: "No, not that MassFinder, the other one"

**Verdict:** The name no longer fits and creates active brand confusion.

### Option B: Descriptive compound name

Examples: "ParishGuide," "CatholicCompanion," "FaithHome," "ParishKeeper"

**Pros:**
- Clear what it does
- Some domains available (parishkeeper.app, faithhome.app)

**Cons:**
- Forgettable — sounds like every other generic Catholic app
- Still boxes the product into a functional description
- No emotional resonance
- Aesthetically flat against names like Hallow or Laudate

**Verdict:** Safe but uninspiring. Won't make anyone say "check out this app."

### Option C: Single evocative word (recommended)

This is the pattern that works. One word that captures a feeling, a space, or a posture — not a function.

#### Top candidates (all with .app domain likely available):

**1. Sacristy** — sacristy.app ✅ AVAILABLE
- The room in a church where sacred vessels, vestments, and liturgical items are prepared and stored
- Metaphor: "everything you need for the liturgical life, gathered in one place"
- Every Catholic knows the word; it's physical, tangible, real
- Captures both practical (schedules, directions) and devotional (prayer tools, formation)
- Not Mass-specific — encompasses the full sacramental and devotional life
- Sophisticated without being obscure
- **Risk:** Some may find it too "back-of-church" / utilitarian. A sacristy is functional, not beautiful.

**2. Vigil** — vigil.app (status uncertain, DNS lookup error — may be available)
- "Keeping watch" — the posture of the faithful
- Resonates: Easter Vigil, Saturday vigil Mass, prayer vigil, "keep vigil"
- Captures the app's daily-briefing quality (Today zone) and its contemplative tools
- Short, strong, easy to say
- **Risk:** Also means "protest vigil" in secular usage. Slightly somber tone.

**3. Introit** — introit.app ✅ AVAILABLE
- The entrance antiphon that begins the Mass — literally "the entering in"
- Perfect metaphor: this app is your entry point into parish life and faith practice
- Beautiful word, musical associations
- **Risk:** Many Catholics (especially younger/returning) won't know this word. Pronunciation unclear to non-liturgists.

**4. Adsum** — adsum.app ✅ AVAILABLE
- Latin: "I am here" / "I am present"
- What a priest says at ordination when his name is called
- Captures presence: being present at Mass, being present in prayer, being present in your parish
- Short, punchy, memorable
- **Risk:** Nobody knows this word outside of seminarians and the liturgically deep. Pronunciation barrier. Sounds like a tech startup.

**5. Orare** — orare.app (registered, no DNS records — may be parked)
- Latin: "to pray" (root of "oration," "oratory")
- Simple, direct, beautiful
- **Risk:** Probably parked by a speculator. Narrows perception to prayer-only.

## Recommendation: **Sacristy**

**Why Sacristy wins for this app:**

1. **It's a room, not an action.** The app isn't just about finding or praying — it's a place you go to prepare. A sacristy is where you get ready for the sacred. The app prepares you: for Mass (service finder), for Confession (Examen + guide), for prayer (Rosary, Stations), for the liturgical season (seasonal content), for deeper understanding (CCC, Bible, Faith Guides).

2. **It scales.** You can put anything in a sacristy. Adding the Summa? Fits. Adding a prayer intention counter? Fits. Chant audio? Fits. The name never constrains future scope.

3. **It signals Catholic identity without excluding.** A returning Catholic hears "Sacristy" and knows this is Catholic. A curious non-Catholic finds the word intriguing. It's neither generic ("FaithApp") nor intimidatingly academic ("Introit").

4. **It serves all three demographics:**
   - **Older parishioners (50+):** They know exactly what a sacristy is. Many have been in one. The name signals respect for tradition.
   - **Phone-native younger adults:** The single-word pattern matches the apps they use (Hallow, Calm, Notion). It's brandable, looks good on a home screen.
   - **Middle group (35-55):** Recognizes the word, appreciates the craft of the naming. "Sacristy — that's clever."

5. **The domain is available.** sacristy.app shows dns_nxdomain — not registered. This is rare for a real English word on a premium TLD.

6. **It has visual identity potential.** A sacristy door, a golden chalice, liturgical vestment fabric, a key — all strong iconographic options that fit the Apple HIG aesthetic already in use.

7. **The tagline writes itself.** "Sacristy — Prepare for the Sacred" or simply "Sacristy — Catholic Life, Gathered."

**What to keep from "MassFinder":**
- The cross-and-gold icon aesthetic
- The "Western New England" regional identity (can be a subtitle)
- The manifest short_name can stay "Sacristy" (8 chars, fits perfectly)

**Migration path:**
- Phase 1: Secure sacristy.app domain (~$12-14/yr for .app)
- Phase 2: Update manifest, meta tags, header wordmark, OG tags
- Phase 3: Set up Vercel custom domain (sacristy.app → existing deployment)
- Phase 4: Add a "formerly MassFinder" note for 3-6 months for existing users
- Total effort: ~2-3 hours of code changes + domain purchase

## Alternative if Sacristy doesn't resonate

If "Sacristy" feels too utilitarian or too "back room," **Vigil** is the runner-up — but verify domain availability first. It has a warmer, more contemplative tone ("keep vigil") but the secular "protest vigil" connotation is a consideration.

If you want something nobody has to look up and that immediately signals what the app does while still being brandable: **"Parish"** as a standalone name (parish.app is almost certainly taken, but worth checking). The word has evolved in Catholic vernacular to mean not just "administrative unit" but "community, home, place of belonging."
